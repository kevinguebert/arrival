Getting Started

Download the latest .dmg from GitHub Releases. Open the disk image, drag Arrival into your Applications folder, and launch it.

First launch: opening an unsigned app

Because Arrival is distributed outside the Mac App Store, macOS may show a warning that it "cannot be opened because the developer cannot be verified."

  1. 1. Locate Arrival.app in your Applications folder (do not double-click).
  2. 2. Right-click (or Control-click) the app icon and choose Open from the menu.
  3. 3. Click Open again in the dialog that appears. macOS will remember your choice.

Once launched, look for the Arrival icon in your menu bar — a small route icon near the clock. If you don't see it, check System Settings → Control Center and make sure menu bar apps are visible.

Setting Up

Before Arrival can show you traffic data, you need to configure at least one route. Click the Arrival menu bar icon and open Preferences.

Enter your addresses

In the Routes tab, add a name for the route (e.g. "Home to Office") then type your origin and destination addresses. Arrival uses Apple Maps or Mapbox to resolve addresses — full street addresses work best.

Configure your commute schedule

Set the days and times you commute. Arrival uses your schedule to show the right direction — heading out in the morning, heading home in the evening — so the menu bar always shows the trip that matters right now.

Using Arrival

Arrival checks traffic in the background and puts everything you need in the menu bar and dropdown panel.

Menu bar icon

The menu bar shows your current travel time (e.g. 22 min) with a color that matches traffic conditions. Green means you're good to go.

Traffic moods

Green — Clear. Traffic is flowing freely. Your commute is on schedule.
Amber — Moderate. Some slowdowns ahead. Leave a few minutes early if you can.
Red — Heavy. Significant congestion on your route. Consider waiting it out or taking an alternate path.

Routes & direction detection

If you have multiple routes, Arrival cycles through them in the menu bar. The dropdown lists all your routes with current travel times. It picks the right direction based on your commute schedule — no need to flip between "to work" and "back home" yourself.

Incidents

Accidents, construction, and road closures on your route show up in the dropdown below your travel time. You'll see how bad each one is so you can decide whether to reroute.

Map Window

Click the map preview in the Arrival dropdown to open a floating map window. It stays on top of your other windows so you can keep an eye on your route without switching away from what you're doing.

Route with congestion overlay

Your route is drawn on the map with color-coded traffic — green where things are moving, amber where it's slowing down, red where it's backed up. You can see exactly which stretch of road is the problem.

Route selection sync

The map window stays in sync with whichever route is selected in the dropdown. Switch routes in the panel and the map updates automatically.

The map window works with Apple Maps out of the box. To see per-segment congestion coloring on the route (green/amber/red bands showing exactly where traffic is backed up), add a free Mapbox API key in Preferences.

Preferences

Open Preferences from the Arrival menu bar icon. All settings are stored locally on your Mac.

Polling interval

How often Arrival checks for new traffic data. Shorter intervals keep things more current but use more network. Default is every 5 minutes.

Maps provider: Apple Maps vs Mapbox

Arrival uses Apple Maps by default — works right away with no setup. You can switch to Mapbox if you want more detailed traffic data:

  • Apple Maps The default. No API key needed. Provides live travel times, route paths, and traffic moods.
  • Mapbox Optional. Adds color-coded congestion on each road segment, traffic incident alerts, and better baseline estimates. Free tier covers 100k requests/month — sign up at mapbox.com and paste your key in Preferences → General → Traffic Provider.

Launch at login

Turn this on and Arrival starts up when you log in to your Mac. One less thing to remember before your commute.

Preferred maps app

When you hit "Get Directions" from the dropdown, Arrival opens your go-to navigation app. Pick from Apple Maps, Google Maps, or Waze.

Troubleshooting

Common questions and fixes.

"macOS says the app is from an unidentified developer"

This is expected for apps distributed outside the App Store. Right-click (or Control-click) the app icon in Finder and choose Open. Then click Open again in the dialog. macOS will remember your decision from then on.

"The app shows '--' instead of a time"

This usually means Arrival couldn't fetch traffic data. Try the following:

  • • Check that your origin and destination addresses are valid and resolve correctly in Preferences.
  • • Verify your Mac has an active internet connection.
  • • If using Mapbox, confirm your API key is entered correctly and has not expired.

"How do I use Mapbox instead of Apple Maps?"

Sign up for a free account at mapbox.com and create a public access token. Then open Arrival Preferences, go to the Mapbox section, and paste your token. Arrival will switch to the Mapbox provider automatically.

"How do I make it launch at startup?"

Open Preferences and toggle on Launch at Login. Arrival uses macOS Login Items, so it will start silently in the background whenever you log in.

Updates

Arrival uses Sparkle to handle updates. When a new version drops, you'll get a prompt inside the app — no need to check GitHub yourself.

If you prefer to update manually, every release is also published on GitHub Releases. Download the latest .dmg, drag the new version to Applications, and replace the old one.

Keep it up to date

Updates bring better traffic accuracy, new features, and fixes for the latest macOS versions. Sparkle will let you know when one's ready.
